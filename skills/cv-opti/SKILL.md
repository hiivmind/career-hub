# CV Optimization for Dual-AI Hiring

Optimize your CV to pass both employer AI systems (ATS screening + fraud detection) while maintaining authentic, verifiable claims that survive technical interviews.

## Understanding the Dual-AI Challenge

Modern hiring deploys two opposing AI systems:

**ATS Screening** (Applicant Tracking System)
- 99% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS
- 75% of resumes rejected before human review
- Scans for keywords, formatting compliance, section structure
- Instant rejection for parsing errors

**Fraud Detection** (Employer Verification)
- 95% accuracy identifying fabricated claims
- Cross-references CV against LinkedIn, company records, public databases
- Technical interviews test every claim on your CV
- Single inconsistency triggers fraud investigation flags

Your CV must satisfy both simultaneously: optimized for machine parsing while completely honest and verifiable through multiple sources.

## Core Principles Applied

### For ATS Optimization
- Use exact keywords extracted from job description
- Maintain clean, simple formatting (no tables, graphics, or special characters)
- Quantify every achievement with specific numbers
- Use standard section headers (PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE, CORE COMPETENCIES, etc.)
- Ensure content parses left-to-right, top-to-bottom
- Limit to 1-2 pages (ATS truncates longer documents)

### For Fraud Detection
- Make every claim independently verifiable through multiple sources
- Ensure CV aligns perfectly with LinkedIn profile and public records
- Use specific numbers that can be corroborated (timeframes, percentages, costs)
- Avoid unverifiable adjectives ("expert," "pioneered," "revolutionized")
- Be prepared to discuss every bullet for 30 minutes in technical interview
- Address employment gaps with coherent, honest context

### For Human Recruiters
- Tell a coherent professional narrative showing growth
- Demonstrate clear progression and increasing impact
- Show business context and outcomes, not just activities
- Balance keyword optimization with readability
- Proof-read obsessively (spelling errors = automatic disqualification)
